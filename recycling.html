Revive Recyling

Why Recycling Matters

Recycling isn't just about bins and trucks—it's a vital part of conserving natural resources, saving energy, and protecting our ecosystems from pollution. When we recycle correctly, we give materials a new life and reduce the strain on our planet

Your Simple Recycling Guide

Follow these basic rules to recycle right, every time.

  1. Empty, Clean & Dry

    Rinse out food containers and bottles.

    Why? Contaminated items can spoil entire batches of recycling, sending them to the landfill.

  2. Know Your Plastics (#1 & #2)

    Focus on plastic bottles and containers marked with #1 (PET) and #2 (HDPE). These are the most commonly accepted plastics.

    Why? Other numbers (#3-#7) have very limited recycling markets.

  3. Keep it Loose!

    Never bag your recyclables. Place them loose in the bin.

    Why? Plastic bags get tangled in sorting machinery, causing shutdowns.

  4. When in Doubt, Throw it Out

    If you're unsure if something is recyclable, it's better to put it in the trash.

    Why? "Wishcycling" does more harm than good by contaminating the stream.

Recycling Myths vs Facts

Let's clear out the confusion and recycle smarter.

Myths Facts
❌"Anything with a recycling symbol can be recycled."
✅The symbol only identifies the plastic type. Local facilities determine what they can process. Always check your local guidelines.
❌"Pizza boxes are recyclable."
✅Only if they are clean! Grease and cheese contaminate paper fibers. Recycle the clean top lid and compost/trash the greasy bottom.
❌"It's okay to recycle small items like bottle caps."
✅Small items often fall through sorting machines. Check local rules—some areas ask you to keep caps on plastic bottles, while others want them off.
❌"Recycling uses more energy than it saves."
✅Recycling aluminum saves 95% of the energy needed to make new metal. Recycling almost always results in a net energy and resource savings.
